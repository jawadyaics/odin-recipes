Chicken Biryani



Description

Behold! An authentic Chicken Biryani recipe with simple, easy-to-follow instructions (no curveballs!) and mouthwatering, traditional Pakistani and Indian flavor. This recipe includes tips on how to get fluffy rice, tender chicken, and the distinct biryani taste. Tested to perfection!

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 500g (bone-in pieces for the best flavor)
  • Basmati Rice: 2 cups (long-grain, soaked for 30 minutes)
  • Yogurt: 1 cup (whisked)
  • Onions: 3 large (thinly sliced and fried until crispy/golden brown)
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Green Chilies: 3–4 (slit)
  • Whole Spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 green cardamoms, 1 black cardamom, 4 cloves, 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Ground Spices: 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tbsp red chili powder, 2 tbsp Biryani masala, 1 tsp garam masala
  • Fresh Herbs: 1/2 cup each of chopped mint and cilantro
  • Fats: 4 tbsp Ghee or Oil
  • Saffron/Color: A pinch of saffron soaked in 1/4 cup warm milk (or a drop of food coloring)
  • Acid: Juice of half a lemon

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, mix the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, half of the fried onions, lemon juice, mint, cilantro, and all the ground spices. Let it rest for at least 1 hour (overnight is better if you’re a planner).
  2. Par-boil the Rice: Bring a large pot of water to a boil with salt and the whole spices. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done (it should still have a slight "bite" to it). Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken Base: Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot (or a Handi). Add the marinated chicken and cook over medium-high heat until the chicken is no longer pink and the oil starts to separate from the gravy.
  4. The Layering Act: Turn the heat to low. Level the chicken at the bottom of the pot. Spread the par-boiled rice evenly over the chicken.
  5. Garnish the Layers: Sprinkle the remaining fried onions, extra mint, cilantro, and the saffron-infused milk over the top of the rice.
  6. The Dum (Steam): Seal the pot tightly with a lid (you can use aluminum foil or a dough seal to keep the steam in). Cook on the lowest heat possible for 15–20 minutes.
  7. Rest and Fluff: Turn off the heat and let the Biryani sit, undisturbed, for another 10 minutes. When you're ready, use a flat spoon to gently fluff the rice from the sides, mixing the white grains with the spiced chicken.
